How to Evaluate a Website Before Using it in Research

Before citing a website in academic research, students should evaluate it carefully.


Here are five important criteria:

1. Author Authority

Check if the author is identified and qualified.

2. Publication Date

Ensure the information is updated and relevant.

3. Domain Type

Websites ending in .edu, .gov, and .org are generally more credible.

4. References and Citations

Reliable websites provide supporting evidence.

5. Cross-Verification

Always compare information with other trusted sources.


Based on student responses, cross-checking information significantly reduces research errors.
